Zillow Home‑Price Prediction Using Public Housing & Demographic Data¶

Leane Steyn, Lillian Paul, Alex Lang

Business Problem and Goal Analysis¶

Zillow is one of the largest real‑estate marketplaces in the U.S., offering home‑value estimates (Zestimates) and market insights. Accurately predicting home prices is challenging due to regional economic differences, demographic shifts, and market volatility.
This project uses Zillow Home Value Index (ZHVI) data and U.S. Census ACS demographic indicators to identify key drivers of home‑price variation and build predictive models for estimating future home values.

Goal: Build 2–3 machine‑learning models to predict home values and interpret which features matter most.

Business challenge¶

Zillow’s pricing accuracy directly affects user trust, platform engagement, and real‑estate decision‑making.
Home prices fluctuate due to income levels, population growth, education, housing supply, and regional economic conditions.
Improving predictive accuracy helps Zillow:

  • Provide more reliable Zestimates
  • Support buyers and sellers
  • Strengthen investor insights

Industry Context¶

The U.S. housing market is influenced by macroeconomic factors such as interest rates, migration patterns, and local affordability. Public datasets like ACS provide essential demographic context.

DataSet:¶

  • ZHVI (Zillow Home Value Index): Monthly home‑value estimates by region.
  • ACS Demographic Data: Income, education, population, housing characteristics.

We merge these datasets at the county or metro level to create a modeling dataset.

Modeling Approach

We treat this as a regression problem, predicting the log of county‑level home values.

Models used:

  1. Linear Regression (baseline, interpretable)
  2. Lasso Regression (regularization and feature selection)
  3. Random Forest Regressor (nonlinear relationships and interactions)

Evaluation Metrics:

  • RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error)
  • MAE (Mean Absolute Error)
  • R² (Coefficient of Determination)

Results and interpretation:

After incorporating the additional demographic features, the models show varied performance. The Linear Regression model yielded an RMSE of 0.169, an MAE of 0.152, and an R-squared of 0.184. The Lasso Regression model, with its regularization, performed similarly, showing an RMSE of 0.183, an MAE of 0.164, and an R-squared of 0.044. Unfortunately, the Random Forest Regressor model performed the worst, with an RMSE of 0.208, an MAE of 0.170, and a negative R-squared of -0.230. This indicates that while Linear and Lasso Regression models now have positive R-squared values, suggesting a slight improvement over simply predicting the mean, their overall predictive power remains quite low. The Random Forest model's negative R-squared implies it's still performing worse than a basic mean-prediction baseline. Overall, the current set of features and models are not strongly predicting the log of home values at the state level.

Conclusions & Recommendations¶

Best Model:
Random Forest achieved the best performance (lowest RMSE, highest R²), suggesting that nonlinear relationships and interactions are important in explaining home values.

Key Drivers:

  • Higher median income is associated with higher home values.
  • Higher education levels correlate with higher home values.
  • Higher poverty rates tend to be associated with lower home values.
  • Owner‑occupied housing rates and population also play meaningful roles.

Business Recommendations:

  • Zillow could incorporate more demographic and socioeconomic features into its pricing models to improve accuracy.
  • Regional models could be tailored based on local income, education, and housing characteristics.
  • Communicating the drivers of estimates can increase user trust in Zestimates.

Limitations:

  • County‑level aggregation may hide within‑county variation.
  • We did not include macroeconomic variables like interest rates.
  • Data quality depends on ACS sampling and ZHVI methodology.

Next Steps:

  • Add interest‑rate and employment data (FRED, BLS).
  • Explore time‑series forecasting of ZHVI.
